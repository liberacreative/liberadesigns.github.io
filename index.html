Library Newsletters

Layout / Image Manipulation / Selection

The Problem

  • I was tasked with formatting City of Sydney Library's 3 bi-monthly newsletter editions for a contracted period.
  • Each newsletter had to accommodate for shifting, often lengthy amounts of text and imagery, as well as engage relevant demographics with respect to the City of Sydney style guide.

The Solution

  • Meticulous attention was given to space, alignment and typesetting to accommodate for shifting amounts of content within each newsletter.
  • Graphical icons (e.g. calendar legend, competency rating) were moreover utilised when relevant, to simplify layout and reduce textual clutter.

Library Posters: Adults

Layout / Image Manipulation / Selection

The Problem

  • For a contracted period, I was tasked with formatting the majority of City of Sydney Library's event posters to be displayed at Library branches.
  • Such posters were produced in high-volume, with the Library needing approximately 20-40 different posters per month to promote events for Adults, Youth and Late Night Library.

The Solution

  • In keeping with the high-volume nature of this project, I introduced a grid (i.e. structured type columns) to accommodate for large amounts of variable content.
  • The grid essentially allowed for different amounts of event text and imagery to be slotted in quickly and consistently, in effect, maintaining the same professional structure for each poster.

Sustainability Reports

Layout / Template Design / Style Guide / Photography

The Problem

  • A group of UTS students and I were tasked with designing PDF progress reports for the Sustainability Department of The University of Technology, Sydney.
  • The original reports were unengaging visually, and consisted of dry information, thus, limiting their ability to spread information to corporate stakeholders and a wider university audience.

The Solution

  • In the redesigned reports, information is broken down and displayed visually through infographics and tables, thus reducing the amount of text a reader must sift through.
  • A visual hierarchy composed of subheadings, highlighted quotes and enlarged statistics moreover makes content skimmable.
  • A style guide was created for all reports, encouraging the consistent application of a simple, modern style that appealed to a broad demographic.

OLOOQ

Josef Adlao Copywriting

Branding / Style Guide / Template Design

The Problem

  • Josef approached me to design a visual identity for his business, specialising in copywriting for B2B (business to business) and high-tech/scientific companies.
  • He wanted his brand to reflect "professionalism" and "astuteness", as well as convey his high-tech/scientific specialisation.

The Solution

  • I sought to visually reference familiar iconography within the scientific field (e.g. DNA and hexagonal molecules) to highlight Josef's copywriting specialisation in technology.
  • This approach aimed to differentiate him from competitors who were more generalist in their approach when it came to targeting copywriting clients.
  • Clean shapes/lines, blue accents, and typography set in all caps, aim to express the company's authority as a professional copywriting business.
