ABOUT ME

I'm a graphic designer who has an innate capacity for visual communication. My conceptual approach brings the abstract into clear, concrete expression. I'm very meticulous when it comes to details. Careful to ensure that design conveys the right mood and impression.

Interests

I'm passionate about translating ideas into aesthetically-pleasing and meaningful pieces. In my personal works, I have a particular interest in exploring emotion, consciousness and psychology, and, bridging the gap between subjective and objective communication.

Experience

Library Newsletters

Work: layout and imagery selection in line with the City of Sydney's style guide

The City of Sydney Library regularly published three bi-monthly newsletters dedicated to promoting Adult, Youth and Late Night Library events. Meticulous attention to detail was given to the typesetting and layout of each newsletter, as well as selecting and editing graphical content to attract distinct Library audiences.

Library Posters: Adults

Work: layout and imagery selection in line with the City of Sydney's style guide

Before Refinement:

The above posters were produced in high volume, with the City of Sydney Library needing approximately 10-40 different posters per month to promote events for adults and Late Night Library. In keeping with the high-volume nature of this project, I introduced a grid (i.e. type columns, see before refinement samples) to accomodate for increasing event text and to foster flexibility, structure and consistency in layout. Care was also taken to select images that clearly encapsulate an event to a wide audience.

Sustainability Reports

Work: template design, style guide, photography

A group of UTS students and I were tasked with designing PDF progress reports for the Sustainability Department of The University of Technology, Sydney. Four primary colours were selected to differentiate one plan from another, while unification between each report was also achieved by utilising such colours throughout each plan. The plans aimed to maintain a simple and professional feel for the Department's corporate stakeholders, yet still remain approachable for a wider university audience.

Landing Page & Branding

Work: layout, style guide, logo, photography

OLOOQ is a newly-conceived website which serves as a search engine and price comparison site purely for organic products. OLOOQ's logo was designed as such to quickly convey the main function of the site as a search engine. I aimed to create a visually appealing landing page that would directly mark the site's main features, and entice individuals to enter and try the site. Fresh produce photography interplays with simple type, to give the brand a fresh, yet organic/rustic feel, true to the authenticity of organic products.
